Manual

  1. First add your Keywords, multiple keywords have to be ";" seperated (CSV).
  2. Then please select from which website the paper shall be fetched.
    • Google scholar: Keyword Paper Miner is optimised for this selection but the abstract of the fetched papers is not complete.
    • PubMed: The evaluation measures are not working for this selection. You can choose PubMed you are interested in the whole abstract.
  3. Then you can choose which parameter matters the most for you:
    • K-Score: This is explained in detail further below.

      NOTE: The calculation of the K-Score is a bit more time consuming than the other evaluation measures.

    • Citations: This ranks the paper by citations.
    • Year: This ranks the papers by the publishing year.
  4. Next you can specify which of the provided factors shall be weighted when calculating the K-Score. This is as well explained in detail further down.
  5. Then choose how many papers should be returned for each keyword.

    NOTE: The more paper you want, the slower longer the application takes to execute.
    When choosing the K-Score as your Scoring System, you can estimate the running time by multiplying the expected amount by 1.5 seconds. Example: For 2 keywords each 100 papers: (2 * 100 * 1) / 60sec = 3.3 min

  6. When you have searched for the keywords, you can switch between the different pages without deleting your results.
